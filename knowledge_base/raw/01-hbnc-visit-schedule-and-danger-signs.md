# HBNC Visit Schedule and Danger Signs for Newborns

## What is HBNC?

Home Based Newborn Care (HBNC) is a government program in India where trained ASHA workers visit newborns at home to assess health, provide counselling to families, and identify babies and mothers needing urgent referral to health facilities. HBNC reduces newborn deaths by catching danger signs early and ensuring sick newborns reach primary health centers (PHCs) for treatment. Each ASHA visit follows a structured schedule with specific checks timed to the newborn's age.

---

## HBNC Visit Schedule

| Visit # | Day of Life | Primary Focus | Typical Timing |
|---------|------------|---------------|-----------------|
| Visit 1 | Day 1-2 | Initial assessment, feeding help, cord care, mother's recovery | Within 24 hours of birth if hospital delivery; within 48 hours if home birth |
| Visit 2 | Day 3 | Feeding check, jaundice risk assessment, cord and skin review | Day 3 of life |
| Visit 3 | Day 7 | Cord separation, feeding assessment, mother's health check | Day 7 of life (±1 day acceptable) |
| Visit 4 | Day 14 | Growth check, feeding adequacy, immunization prep | Day 14 of life |
| Visit 5 | Day 28 | Final newborn check, exclusive breastfeeding status, immunization referral | Day 28 of life |

## What to Check: Visit-Day-Specific Monitoring

### **Visit 1 (Day 1-2): Initial Assessment & Support**

**Newborn checks:**
- Ask: Is the baby feeding? How many times in last 24 hours? Any difficulty latching?
- Look: Color of skin — is it yellow? (jaundice on Day 1-2 is concerning)
- Feel: Baby's temperature — use hand on abdomen and chest (should feel warm, not cold)
- Count: Breathing rate — count breaths in 60 seconds (normal is 30-60 per minute at rest; >60 is fast breathing)
- Watch: Is baby active and responsive when awake? Does baby cry with normal strength?
- Check: Cord — is it wet, bleeding, or has foul smell? (cord should be dry, no odor)
- Observe: Urine and stool — has baby passed meconium (dark stool) yet?

**Mother checks:**
- Ask: How is bleeding? (small amount normal; soaking pads is not)
- Ask: Any fever or chills? (temperature >38.5°C is fever)
- Ask: Breasts — pain, hardness, or redness?
- Ask: Mood — is she anxious, sad, or unable to cope?

**Counselling focus:**
- Demonstrate correct breastfeeding position
- Explain cord care (keep dry, expose to air)
- Teach skin-to-skin contact benefits
- Explain when danger signs need referral

---

### **Visit 2 (Day 3): Feeding & Jaundice Focus**

**Newborn checks:**
- Ask: Feeding history — how many times per day? Any improvement from Day 1-2?
- Look: **Skin color carefully** — jaundice (yellow color) is most common problem on Day 3
  - Check in natural daylight
  - If yellow extends to palms/soles or baby looks very yellow, this is serious
- Count: How many wet diapers and stools in last 24 hours? (should be ≥3 wet, ≥3 stools)
- Observe: Activity level, response to mother's voice
- Check: Temperature, breathing rate, cord status

**Mother checks:**
- Assess bleeding, fever, breast problems
- Ask about sleep and mood — Day 3 "baby blues" is common but severe depression needs help

**Counselling focus:**
- Assess breastfeeding effectiveness (baby losing weight is red flag)
- Explain jaundice risk and when it's dangerous
- Reinforce cord care and temperature maintenance

---

### **Visit 3 (Day 7): Cord Separation & Feeding Adequacy**

**Newborn checks:**
- Check: Cord — is it separated? If still attached, is it dry with no signs of infection (redness, oozing, foul smell)?
- Assess: Feeding — review details of last 24 hours
  - How many times? Duration at each breast?
  - Are there any cries suggesting inadequate milk?
- Look: Jaundice — by Day 7, physiological jaundice should be improving; if still yellow or worsening, note it
- Feel: Weight — if baby feels lighter/thinner than at birth, there may be feeding problem
- Count: Wet diapers (should be ≥6 by Day 7) and stools
- Observe: General activity, alertness, response to stimulation

**Mother checks:**
- Bleeding should be decreasing; lochia (discharge) should smell normal, not foul
- Any fever, infected feeling, or severe pain?
- Breast engorgement resolving? Any cracked, painful, or infected areas?
- Mood check — is mother coping?

**Counselling focus:**
- Reinforce exclusive breastfeeding (no water, no other feeds)
- If feeding inadequate, demonstrate expressing and cup feeding
- Explain that cord being attached until Day 10-14 is normal
- Identify and support any maternal depression

---

### **Visit 4 (Day 14): Growth & Feeding Adequacy**

**Newborn checks:**
- Compare: Is baby bigger/healthier than Day 7? Any visible improvement?
- Weight: Ideally, baby should be regaining birth weight by now
- Feeding: How many times? Is mother confident? Any pain?
- Wet diapers & stools: Should remain ≥6 wet and ≥3-4 stools
- Activity: Alertness, muscle tone, response to voice and touch
- Breathing, temperature, color — routine checks

**Mother checks:**
- Lochia should be much reduced; no foul smell
- No fever, no severe pain
- Breasts should be settled (not engorged)
- Emotional state — any persistent sadness or anxiety?

**Counselling focus:**
- Confirm exclusive breastfeeding continues
- Prepare mother for immunizations (first vaccines at birth and 6 weeks)
- Check readiness for any family planning counseling if appropriate

---

### **Visit 5 (Day 28): Final Assessment & Referral**

**Newborn checks:**
- General health: Baby should look healthy, alert, active
- Growth: Weight regain achieved? Baby should look visibly heavier than at birth
- Feeding: Still exclusive breastfeeding? Adequate production? Mother satisfied?
- Wet diapers & stools: Pattern should be well-established (≥6 wet, ≥3 stools)
- Activity & interaction: Responsive, engaging with mother
- Breathing, temperature, color — final checks
- Cord: Any late complications? (should be fully separated by now)

**Mother checks:**
- Final check on bleeding, fever, breast health
- Mental health — any depression or anxiety needing ongoing support?
- Knowledge: Does she know when to seek care for baby after 28 days?

**Counselling focus:**
- Reinforce exclusive breastfeeding until 6 months
- Ensure awareness of next immunization at 6 weeks
- Provide information on newborn danger signs to watch after HBNC ends
- If any ongoing concerns, link to village health guide or regular ANM follow-up

## Universal Newborn Danger Signs (All Ages 0-28 Days)

**ANY of these signs = immediate referral to PHC needed**

### **Feeding Problems (ஊட்ட சிக்கல்)**
- Baby not feeding at all, or very weak/sleepy feeding
- Inability to latch onto breast
- Repeated vomiting (more than 2-3 times)
- Persistent refusal of feeds
- Signs of severe dehydration: sunken fontanelle (soft spot), very dry mouth, no tears, lethargy

### **Breathing Problems (சுவாசக் கஷ்டம்)**
- Fast breathing: ≥60 breaths per minute (count for full 60 seconds at rest when calm)
- Grunting sound when breathing
- Nostrils flaring with each breath
- Chest indrawing (ribs or breastbone sinking in during breathing)
- Apnea (baby stops breathing suddenly, becomes blue)

### **Temperature Problems (வெப்பநிலை பிரச்சனை)**
- **Very high fever:** ≥38.5°C (newborn fever is always serious; any fever in first 7 days needs urgent referral)
- **Severe hypothermia:** baby feels very cold, limp, difficult to wake, temperature <35.5°C (even if baby appears sleepy, this is dangerous)

### **Cord Problems (தொப்புளி வழியான தொற்று)**
- Redness, swelling, warmth, or pus around cord or umbilicus
- Foul-smelling discharge from cord stump
- Bleeding from cord
- Cord not separated by Day 14
- Mother notices increased warmth or redness spreading

### **Jaundice (மஞ்சள் நோய்)**
- **Visible yellow color on Day 1-2:** any jaundice in first 48 hours is pathological (not normal)
- **Yellow color extending to palms, soles, or entire body** (by any day)
- **Yellow color of whites of eyes (sclera)**
- Baby becoming increasingly lethargic or difficult to feed with jaundice present

### **Lethargy or Abnormal Behavior (சோர்வு அல்லது அசாதாரண நடவடிக்கை)**
- Baby not responding to mother's voice or touch
- Extremely difficult to wake, or staying asleep for unusually long periods
- Weak cry (sounds different from normal cry)
- Seizures or stiffening of body
- Inability to lift head when prompted (by Day 7+)

## Maternal Danger Signs (Mother of Newborn)

**ANY of these signs = mother needs immediate PHC referral**

### **Excessive Bleeding (அதிக இரத்தப்போக்கு)**
- Soaking through pads faster than one pad per hour
- Passing blood clots larger than golf ball
- Bleeding worsens after Day 3-5 (should decrease over time)

### **Fever (காய்ச்சல்)**
- Temperature ≥38.5°C, especially with chills or sweating
- Fever in first 48 hours after birth (risk of infection)
- Fever returning after improving

### **Breast Problems (மார்பக சிக்கல்)**
- Severe pain not improving with feeding position changes
- Hard, red, swollen area in one breast with fever (mastitis)
- Cracked, bleeding nipples with pus or infection

### **Mental Health Problems (மानसिक स्वास्थ्य)**
- Severe sadness or inability to stop crying
- Unable or unwilling to care for baby
- Thoughts of harming herself or baby (dangerous postpartum depression)
- Severe anxiety making normal activities impossible

### **Other Serious Signs**
- Severe abdominal pain not explained by normal cramps
- Foul-smelling vaginal discharge (infection)
- Loss of consciousness or severe dizziness
- Difficulty urinating or burning urination lasting days
- Leg swelling with pain and warmth (blood clot risk)

## What "Serious" Means & When to Refer

### **Immediate 108 Ambulance (Life-Threatening Emergency)**
Call 108 emergency ambulance if:
- Newborn has seizures, severe respiratory distress, apnea, or severe jaundice
- Mother has severe bleeding, unconsciousness, or thoughts of self-harm
- Any sign of sepsis: fever + lethargy + poor feeding together
- Cord with spreading infection (cellulitis)

**Do not delay transport. Do not wait for PHC appointment.**

### **PHC Walk-In Referral (Urgent, Same Day)**
Take to PHC immediately if:
- Feeding problems with signs of dehydration
- Moderate jaundice on Day 3+ or any jaundice on Day 1-2
- Persistent high fever
- Breathing difficulty without respiratory distress
- Cord infection without spreading
- Mother with fever or heavy bleeding (not life-threatening emergency, but needs evaluation)

**Message to family:** "Baby needs to see doctor today. Please go to PHC now."

### **Planned Referral (Next Day or Scheduled)**
- Weight loss >10% from birth weight (needs evaluation but not emergency)
- Feeding issues without dehydration (may benefit from counseling or support)
- Jaundice suspected but not confirmed (assessment and testing needed)

### **Follow-Up Advice (No Referral, Monitor)**
- Normal physiological jaundice in first week (provide breastfeeding support, plan next visit)
- Slight weight loss in first 3-5 days (normal if feeding improving)
- Minimal cord concerns without infection (keep monitoring, review care)

## Tamil Terminology for 6 Core Newborn Danger Signs

**For counselling consistency, use these exact Tamil terms:**

1. **சுவாசக் கஷ்டம்** (Suvaasa Kastam) = Breathing difficulty / Fast breathing
2. **மஞ்சள் நோய்** (Manjai Noi) = Jaundice / Yellow baby disease
3. **சோர்வு / விழித்திராமை** (Sorvu / Vizhithirai Mai) = Lethargy / Not waking / Very sleepy
4. **ஊட்ட சிக்கல்** (Oota Sikkhal) = Feeding problem / Not eating
5. **தொப்புளி வழியான தொற்று** (Thopuli Vazhiyaana Thottru) = Cord infection / Umbilical infection
6. **வெப்பநிலை பிரச்சனை** (Veppa Nilai Pirachchanai) = Temperature problem / Fever / Cold baby

## Summary: ASHA's Role in HBNC

1. **Identify:** Use the visit schedule to check newborn and mother health
2. **Educate:** Counsel on breastfeeding, cord care, and danger signs
3. **Refer:** If any danger sign present, refer immediately (108 for emergencies, PHC for urgent)
4. **Document:** Record findings and actions in HBNC register
5. **Follow-up:** If referred, confirm family reached facility and baby received care
