# Followup Schedule and Escalation Logic for HBNC

## Purpose of This Document

This document defines **deterministic rules** for scheduling followup visits and escalations in HBNC. These are not suggestions or opinions — they are the clinical logic that ensures every newborn receives appropriate follow-up based on their specific situation. The counselling system uses these rules to generate a structured followup plan as part of each ASHA visit response.

---

## Standard HBNC Followup Schedule (Healthy Newborn, No Danger Signs)

For a newborn with **no danger signs**, **normal weight**, **adequate feeding**, and **normal vital signs**:

| Current Visit | Visit Day | Next Visit Day | Days Until Next Visit | Conducted By | Purpose |
|---------------|-----------|----------------|----------------------|--------------|---------|
| Visit 1 | Day 1-2 | Day 3 | 1-2 days | ASHA (HBNC) | Check feeding, jaundice risk, mother's recovery |
| Visit 2 | Day 3 | Day 7 | 4 days | ASHA (HBNC) | Check feeding adequacy, cord status, weight loss |
| Visit 3 | Day 7 | Day 14 | 7 days | ASHA (HBNC) | Check weight regain, feeding, cord separation |
| Visit 4 | Day 14 | Day 28 | 14 days | ASHA (HBNC) | Check growth, feeding well-established, immunization prep |
| Visit 5 | Day 28 | After Day 28 | HBNC ends | ANM/Health system | Link to ongoing health services (immunization, growth monitoring) |

**Key principle:** Standard schedule assumes baby is well, growing, and feeding adequately. Schedule changes if ANY danger sign present.

---

## Modified Followup Schedule: Danger Sign Cases

When a danger sign is identified, the followup schedule changes based on severity. Use this logic tree:

### **Severity Level 1: IMMEDIATE REFERRAL (Life-Threatening)**

**Condition:** Any critical danger sign present (severe respiratory distress, seizures, severe hypothermia, shock signs, severe bleeding mother, etc.)

**Action:** Call 108 ambulance or take to PHC NOW (do not wait for scheduled visit)

**Followup after referral:**
- ASHA confirms baby reached facility same day
- Confirm diagnosis and treatment
- Plan home followup once baby returns or is discharged
- If baby hospitalized: followup depends on hospital discharge plan

**What to tell mother:** "Your baby needs to go to the hospital RIGHT NOW. Call 108 or go to [PHC name] immediately. Take baby with you for breastfeeding."

---

### **Severity Level 2: URGENT REFERRAL (Same-Day PHC)**

**Condition:** Significant danger sign but baby not critically ill (moderate jaundice, cord infection with discharge, fever ≥38.5°C, poor feeding with dehydration signs, respiratory signs without apnea, etc.)

**Action:** Refer to PHC same day (within hours, not next day)

**Followup after referral:**
- ASHA follows up next day to confirm:
  - Did family reach PHC?
  - What was the diagnosis?
  - What treatment was given?
  - When to return home or next visit?
- If baby discharged same day: Plan followup visit 3-5 days later (not standard schedule)
- If baby hospitalized: Wait for discharge, then plan recovery-focused followup

**What to tell mother:** "Your baby needs to see the doctor at PHC today. You should go now/this afternoon. Take baby and come back to breastfeed. Tell me when you return."

---

### **Severity Level 3: CLOSE MONITORING (Recheck in 2-3 Days)**

**Condition:** Minor/early danger sign present but baby still stable (mild jaundice, early cord redness, slight temperature elevation, borderline fast breathing, feeding establishing but inadequate, etc.)

**Action:** Do NOT refer to PHC now, but schedule recheck visit sooner than standard interval

**Next visit:** 2-3 days (not waiting for standard schedule)

**What to assess at recheck:**
- Is danger sign improving?
- Are vital signs better?
- If YES → return to standard schedule
- If NO or WORSE → escalate to urgent referral (Severity Level 2)

**What to tell mother:** "Your baby has [specific finding]. This is not an emergency now, but I need to check again in 2-3 days. Watch for [specific worsening signs] and tell me immediately if baby develops fever, stops feeding, or has breathing problems."

---

### **Severity Level 4: MONITOR AT NEXT VISIT (Standard Schedule, Heightened Awareness)**

**Condition:** Borderline or very mild concern (baby appears slightly sleepy but feeding okay, minimal cord redness, mother anxious but baby doing well, etc.)

**Action:** Continue standard schedule but with focused monitoring

**Next visit:** Standard schedule (do not advance visit)

**What to assess at next visit:**
- Has concern resolved?
- Any worsening?
- Is baby catching up on feeding/weight?

**What to tell mother:** "Baby is okay right now, but I will pay special attention to [specific area] at the next visit. Come on [specific date]. If you notice [specific concerning changes], come to PHC before that."

## Followup Rules by Specific Danger Sign (Core 6 Flags)

Use these rules when a specific danger sign is identified:

### **1. Breathing Problem (सुवाहन कष्ट / சுவாசக் கஷ்டம்)**

**Definition:** Respiratory rate ≥60 at rest, grunting, flaring, or chest indrawing

**Followup logic:**

| Severity | Presentation | Action | Next Visit |
|----------|--------------|--------|-----------|
| Severe | RR ≥80, indrawing, grunting, apnea, cyanosis | 108 ambulance immediately | Depends on hospital |
| Moderate | RR 60-75, grunting or flaring present | PHC same day | Follow hospital/PHC instructions |
| Mild | RR 60-70, no other signs, improving | Recheck in 1-2 days | Improve = return to standard schedule |

**Details:**
- If breathing problem noted on Visit 1 or 2: schedule recheck in 24 hours
- If breathing problem noted on Visit 3+: can recheck in 2 days if stable
- If baby showing any respiratory distress (indrawing, grunting), do NOT wait — refer same day
- Count breathing rate for full 60 seconds at rest (not during feeding/crying) before deciding severity

**Monitor for escalation:**
- Worsening respiratory rate → immediate escalation to 108
- Lethargy + fast breathing → immediate escalation to 108
- No improvement in 24 hours → escalate to PHC referral

---

### **2. Jaundice (மஞ்சள் நோய்)**

**Definition:** Visible yellow color of skin/eyes (extent depends on Day of life)

**Followup logic:**

| Day of Life | Presentation | Action | Next Visit |
|-------------|--------------|--------|-----------|
| Day 1-2 | Any jaundice visible | PHC referral same day | Follow PHC plan (may need lights) |
| Day 3-5 | Yellow on face/trunk, not on limbs | Recheck in 2-3 days | Monitor; if worsening → PHC |
| Day 3-5 | Yellow extending to limbs/palms | PHC referral same day | May need phototherapy |
| Day 5+ | Mild yellow, improving | Recheck at next visit | Monitor trends |
| Day 5+ | Yellow worsening or not improving | PHC referral | May need phototherapy |

**Details:**
- On Visit 2 (Day 3): jaundice is extremely common — assess risk and feeding adequacy
- Feeding support (increase frequency) is primary treatment for physiological jaundice
- If jaundice severe enough to require PHC: recheck 3-5 days after starting treatment
- If jaundice mild and improving with increased breastfeeding: standard schedule is okay
- If jaundice with lethargy or poor feeding: escalate to urgent PHC referral

**Monitor for escalation:**
- Jaundice worsening despite increased breastfeeding → PHC
- Jaundice with lethargy or seizures → 108 ambulance
- Jaundice appearing after Day 7 and worsening → PHC

---

### **3. Lethargy/Poor Feeding (சோர்வு / பசியின்மை)**

**Definition:** Baby very sleepy, hard to wake, OR difficulty feeding/latch, weak suck, OR poor milk transfer

**Followup logic:**

| Severity | Presentation | Action | Next Visit |
|----------|--------------|--------|-----------|
| Severe | Lethargic + fever or hypothermia | 108 ambulance | Hospital |
| Severe | Lethargic + fast breathing | 108 ambulance | Hospital |
| Severe | Lethargic + jaundice | PHC urgent same day | Evaluate for sepsis/bilirubin |
| Moderate | Poor feeding with inadequate wets (<6/day) on Day 3-5 | Recheck in 2-3 days | Teach positioning, increase frequency |
| Moderate | Baby sleepy on normal frequency feeds | Recheck in 2 days | Assess for latch issues, mother stress |
| Mild | Occasional sleepiness but waking for feeds | Standard schedule | Monitor closely, teach stimulation |

**Details:**
- Lethargy combined with OTHER signs (fever, fast breathing) is more concerning than lethargy alone
- If baby very difficult to wake, check temperature first (fever + lethargy = sepsis risk)
- If feeding issue: assess latch quality, teach mother, provide support
- Recheck visit should focus on: Is baby more alert? Are wet diapers increasing? Is weight stabilizing?
- If no improvement at recheck: escalate to PHC for lactation support or evaluation

**Monitor for escalation:**
- Lethargy worsening or baby cannot be roused → 108
- Lethargy + fever → PHC same day
- Lethargy + fast breathing → 108
- No feeding improvement after teaching/support in 3 days → PHC evaluation

---

### **4. Temperature Problem (வெப்பநிலை பிரச்சனை)**

**Definition:** Fever ≥38.5°C OR Hypothermia <35.5°C

**Followup logic:**

| Temperature | Presentation | Action | Next Visit |
|-------------|--------------|--------|-----------|
| Fever ≥39°C | Any newborn with this fever | 108 ambulance | Hospital |
| Fever 38.5-38.9°C | Alone, baby otherwise well | PHC same day | Evaluate for infection source |
| Fever 38.5-38.9°C | With lethargy, fast breathing, poor feeding | 108 ambulance | Hospital |
| Hypothermia <35.5°C | Baby cold, limp, lethargic | 108 ambulance | Hospital/warming |
| Hypothermia 35.5-36°C | Baby cold but responsive | PHC same day | Rewarm, evaluate cause |
| Temperature 36-37.5°C | Low normal, baby appears well | Recheck in 24 hours | Monitor for trend |

**Details:**
- ANY fever in a newborn (especially first week) is concerning — do not wait
- Hypothermia is even more serious than fever — impairs immune function, indicates serious illness
- Temperature measurement matters: use axillary (armpit) for consistency, not rectal if possible
- If fever present on follow-up visit: do not delay referral
- If temperature was borderline at last visit: prioritize temperature check at next visit

**Monitor for escalation:**
- Fever not improving with rest, fluids, antibiotics at PHC → escalate care level
- Hypothermia not responding to rewarming → hospital
- Fever returning after improving → re-evaluate for ongoing infection

---

### **5. Cord Infection (தொப்புளி வழியான தொற்று)**

**Definition:** Redness, swelling, discharge, foul smell at umbilicus

**Followup logic:**

| Stage | Presentation | Action | Next Visit |
|-------|--------------|--------|-----------|
| Severe with sepsis signs | Spreading cellulitis + fever + lethargy | 108 ambulance | Hospital |
| Severe | Cellulitis (widespread redness >2cm), pus, fever ≥38°C | PHC urgent same day | IV antibiotics likely needed |
| Moderate | Increased redness, mild swelling, yellow discharge, low-grade fever | PHC same day | Antibiotics, cord care teach |
| Mild | Slight redness, no discharge, no fever, baby well | Recheck in 2-3 days | Monitor closely for worsening |
| Improving | Previous concern now improving | Standard schedule | Continue cord care, monitor |

**Details:**
- Cord is most common infection site in newborns — high vigilance required
- Any cord with pus or foul smell requires PHC referral (not home care only)
- Mild redness alone with no discharge and no fever can be monitored, but recheck soon
- After PHC referral for cord infection: recheck cord at each visit until completely healed
- Cord should be fully separated and healed by Day 14-21; if not, may indicate infection

**Monitor for escalation:**
- Mild redness worsening → escalate to PHC referral
- Redness with spreading cellulitis → 108 ambulance
- Cord with pus/foul smell appearing after initial redness → escalate urgency
- Cord infection with fever + lethargy → 108 ambulance (sepsis risk)

---

### **6. Lethargy / Abnormal Behavior (சோர்வு / அசாதாரண நடவடிக்கை)**

[This overlaps with #3 but includes broader behavioral concerns]

**Definition:** Baby not responding to stimulation, very difficult to wake, weak cry, seizures, stiffness, or abnormal tone

**Followup logic:**

| Severity | Presentation | Action | Next Visit |
|----------|--------------|--------|-----------|
| Severe | Seizures or loss of consciousness | 108 ambulance | Hospital |
| Severe | Lethargic + fever or hypothermia | 108 ambulance | Hospital |
| Severe | Baby appears "septic" (pale, mottled, limp) | 108 ambulance | Hospital |
| Moderate | Hard to wake, weak response to stimuli | PHC same day | Evaluate for sepsis, metabolic issues |
| Mild | Occasionally sleepy but responds to stimulation | Recheck in 2 days | Monitor for improvement |

**Details:**
- Lethargy is often the ONLY sign of serious infection in newborns (may be no fever)
- Always check temperature when you see lethargy
- Mother's observation is important: "Baby doesn't look right" suggests lethargy
- If baby improves with stimulation and feeds well: may be normal sleepiness
- If baby cannot be roused or weak response: refer to PHC

**Monitor for escalation:**
- Lethargy deepening or baby unresponsive → 108
- Lethargy with fever, fast breathing, or poor feeding → PHC same day or 108
- No improvement in alertness by 24 hours → escalate to PHC

## Followup Rules by Baby Weight Category

### **Normal Birth Weight (≥2.5 kg)**

**Followup:** Standard HBNC schedule applies

| Visit | Day | Details |
|-------|-----|---------|
| Visit 1 | Day 1-2 | Check feeding, temperature, cord, mother recovery |
| Visit 2 | Day 3 | Check feeding, jaundice, weight loss |
| Visit 3 | Day 7 | Check weight regain, feeding, cord separation |
| Visit 4 | Day 14 | Check growth, feeding established |
| Visit 5 | Day 28 | Final check, refer to ANM |

**No weight-specific modifications needed if baby is feeding adequately.**

---

### **Low Birth Weight: 2.0-2.5 kg**

**Followup:** More frequent visits than standard schedule

| Visit | Day | Details | Key Focuses |
|-------|-----|---------|-------------|
| Visit 1 | Day 1-2 | Standard assessment | Watch for feeding difficulty |
| Visit 2 | Day 3 | Earlier assessment (vs Day 3 standard) | Feeding assessment critical |
| Recheck | Day 5-6 | Added visit (not in standard schedule) | Weight loss progress, feeding |
| Visit 3 | Day 8-9 | Slightly later than Day 7 | Weight regain progress |
| Visit 4 | Day 14-16 | Check weight recovery | Should be near birth weight |
| Visit 5 | Day 28 | Standard timing | Confirm sustained growth |

**Rationale:** LBW babies are at higher risk for poor feeding and slow growth. More frequent monitoring helps catch problems early.

**Feeding focus:**
- Assess latch carefully at every visit
- Offer cup feeding teaching early if breastfeeding difficult
- Watch wet diapers closely (may be fewer in LBW baby, but should still be adequate)
- Plan weight check at each visit (critical to monitor trajectory)

**Escalation trigger:** If weight loss is >12% or not regaining by Day 7 → escalate to PHC assessment

---

### **Very Low Birth Weight: 1.5-2.0 kg**

**Followup:** Intensive schedule with frequent monitoring

| Visit | Day | Details | Critical Focus |
|-------|-----|---------|-----------------|
| Visit 1 | Day 1 | Same-day assessment if possible | Feeding capability |
| Visit 2 | Day 2-3 | Very early recheck | Can baby latch? Feeding attempt |
| Recheck | Day 5 | Additional visit | Weight trajectory, feeding adequacy |
| Recheck | Day 7-8 | Check weight regain | Should be stabilizing |
| Recheck | Day 10-12 | Ongoing monitoring | Growth pattern |
| Visit 3 | Day 14-16 | Standard Day 14 assessment | Weight status |
| Visit 4 | Day 21-28 | Final HBNC visit | Growth trajectory |

**Rationale:** Very LBW babies often struggle with feeding and have slower weight gain. Weekly or twice-weekly monitoring is needed.

**Special considerations:**
- May not be able to breastfeed in first week — express milk and cup feed
- Expect slower weight regain trajectory (should start by Day 5-7)
- May need cup feeding training
- Higher risk of infection — monitor temperature, cord, signs carefully

**Strong referral criteria:** Any danger sign + LBW = lower threshold for PHC referral (do not wait for recheck)

---

### **Extremely Low Birth Weight: <1.5 kg**

**Followup:** Likely requires hospital-based care, not home-based HBNC

**Recommendation:** Ensure baby is in appropriate facility with:
- Nutritional support (feeding assessment, possible tube feeding)
- Temperature monitoring (incubator/warmer if available)
- Infection monitoring (frequent vital signs)

**ASHA role:**
- Support mother in expressing milk for baby
- Help coordinate transport if baby is hospitalized
- Follow up once baby is ready for discharge
- Plan intensive HBNC schedule for home care after discharge

**Followup schedule after discharge from hospital:** Same as VLBW schedule above (Day 1, 2-3, 5, 7-8, 10-12, 14-16, 21-28)

---

### **Premature Baby (Born <37 Weeks, But ≥2.0 kg)**

**Followup:** Use corrected age for milestones, but physical assessment is Day of life

**Schedule:**

| Visit | Day of Life | Corrected Age | Details |
|-------|-------------|---------------|---------|
| Visit 1 | Day 1-2 | Very premature | Feeding may not be possible; assess capability |
| Visit 2 | Day 3 | Day 2-3 corrected | May still be expressing milk + cup feeding |
| Recheck | Day 5-7 | Day 4-6 corrected | Assess breastfeeding readiness |
| Visit 3 | Day 10-14 | Day 9-13 corrected | May be transitioning to breastfeeding |
| Visit 4 | Day 21-28 | Day 20-27 corrected | Feeding pattern established |
| Visit 5 | Day 35-42 | Day 34-41 corrected | Ready for regular follow-up |

**Key points:**
- Use **corrected age** to assess feeding readiness (not calendar age)
- Physical visits still happen on **calendar days** (Day 3 of life, Day 7 of life, etc.)
- Use **calendar age** for infection risk and cord care (cord is at risk on calendar days 1-14)
- By Day 42-56 of life, corrected age is old enough for independent feeding

**Feeding timeline by corrected age:**
- <34 weeks corrected: probably cannot breastfeed, use cup feeding
- 34-36 weeks corrected: learning to breastfeed, complement with cup
- ≥36 weeks corrected: should be able to breastfeed effectively

## Followup Rules by Visit Day (What Happens After Each Visit)

### **After Visit 1 (Day 1-2): What is Next Visit?**

**Healthy baby, no danger signs:**
→ Visit 2 scheduled for Day 3

**Danger sign present:**
- **Immediate referral signs** (fever, severe jaundice, respiratory distress, lethargy + fever): PHC now
- **Urgent referral signs** (poor feeding, mild jaundice, cord redness): PHC same day, ASHA follows up next day
- **Recheck in 2-3 days**: Baby has mild concerns but stable

**Special circumstances:**
- **LBW baby (<2.5 kg):** Plan recheck in 2 days instead of 3 days
- **Premature baby:** Plan Visit 2 for calendar Day 3 (not corrected Day 3)
- **Mother with danger signs:** Provide mother referral, ensure baby followup continues

**What to tell mother at Visit 1:**
"I will see your baby again on [specific date, Day 3]. Call me or come to the PHC immediately if: [list danger signs]. Otherwise, I'll be here on [date] for the next check."

---

### **After Visit 2 (Day 3): What is Next Visit?**

**Healthy baby, adequate feeding, no jaundice concern:**
→ Visit 3 scheduled for Day 7

**Danger sign present:**
- **Jaundice concern** (yellow on face/trunk): Recheck in 2-3 days (do not wait for Day 7)
- **Feeding inadequate** (<6 wet diapers, weight loss >10%): Recheck in 2-3 days
- **Cord concern** (redness, discharge, warm): PHC same day or recheck in 2 days
- **Fever/lethargy**: PHC same day or 108 if severe

**Special circumstances:**
- **LBW baby:** May schedule recheck for Day 5-6 instead of waiting for Day 7
- **Very LBW baby:** Plan recheck for Day 5
- **Premature baby:** Still on calendar schedule (Day 7 visit on calendar Day 7, even if corrected age is only Day 5)

**Weight assessment critical at Visit 2:**
- If weight loss >10%: consider recheck in 2 days
- If weight loss 7-10%: monitor, may need feeding support
- If weight loss <7%: normal, continue standard schedule

**What to tell mother at Visit 2:**
"Baby is feeding well [or has some feeding difficulty]. I will see you again on Day 7 [or Day 5 if earlier recheck needed]. Watch for these signs... [list danger signs]."

---

### **After Visit 3 (Day 7): What is Next Visit?**

**Healthy baby, weight regaining, feeding established:**
→ Visit 4 scheduled for Day 14

**Danger sign or concern present:**
- **Weight not regaining** (<7-10% regain from birth weight): Plan recheck in 3-4 days
- **Feeding still inadequate**: Plan recheck in 2-3 days
- **Cord not yet separated or concerning**: Plan recheck in 3-5 days
- **Jaundice still present/worsening**: PHC same day or recheck in 2-3 days
- **Any fever, lethargy, respiratory signs**: Escalate as per severity

**Weight milestones at Day 7:**
- Normal weight baby: Should be approaching birth weight (±5%)
- LBW baby: Should be starting to regain (may still be 7-10% below)
- Very LBW baby: May still be losing, but loss should be slowing

**What to tell mother at Visit 3:**
"Baby is doing well and feeding is improving. I will see you again on Day 14. Your baby is [weight status]. Continue [specific feeding/cord care advice]. Call me if [danger signs]."

---

### **After Visit 4 (Day 14): What is Next Visit?**

**Healthy baby, weight regained, feeding well:**
→ Visit 5 scheduled for Day 28

**Concern present:**
- **Weight still not regained or low**: Plan recheck in 1-2 weeks (do not wait for Day 28)
- **Feeding still difficult**: PHC referral for lactation support, plan recheck in 3-5 days
- **Cord infection**: PHC assessment, plan followup in 3-5 days
- **Any fever, respiratory signs, lethargy**: Escalate as per danger signs

**Weight milestone at Day 14:**
- Normal weight baby: Should have regained birth weight or be within 2-3% of birth weight
- LBW baby: Should be approaching or at birth weight
- Very LBW baby: Should be starting steady regain
- Any baby significantly below birth weight at Day 14: refer to PHC for assessment

**What to tell mother at Visit 4:**
"Your baby is growing well. I will come back on Day 28 for the last HBNC visit. After that, you will go to the ANM [health worker] for baby's regular checks and vaccines. Remember to exclusively breastfeed until 6 months."

---

### **After Visit 5 (Day 28): What is Next?**

**HBNC program ends.** Baby transitions to routine health services.

**Followup pathways:**

**Healthy baby, no concerns:**
→ Refer to ANM for:
- Immunization (6-week vaccine schedule)
- Growth monitoring (regular weighing)
- General health questions
- Link to health system for ongoing care

**Baby with previous concerns (infection treated, weight issue resolving, etc.):**
→ Refer to ANM with note on:
- What was the concern?
- What was the treatment/outcome?
- What should ANM monitor?
- Plan ANM follow-up at 6 weeks (vaccines + growth check)

**Baby with ongoing issues (still not feeding well, growth inadequate, etc.):**
→ **Refer to PHC/hospital for further assessment** before ending HBNC
→ If referral needed: ASHA arranges transport, accompanies mother, ensures baby is seen
→ Plan HBNC continuation if baby hospitalized and returns home, OR link to ANM for home-based management

**What to tell mother at Visit 5:**
"Your baby has completed the home care visits. Now you will take your baby to the ANM on [date] at [location] for the 6-week vaccine and regular weighing. Continue exclusive breastfeeding. Come to PHC anytime if baby seems sick. You can call me if you have questions about feeding or baby care."

**Transition to ANM:**
- Ensure mother knows ANM's name, location, schedule
- Provide HBNC summary to mother (or ensure it reaches ANM)
- Confirm mother's phone number with ANM if possible
- Clarify that PHC is available anytime for acute problems

## What to Communicate to Mother About Followup: Exact Language

### **Standard Followup Communication (Healthy Baby)**

**At Visit 1:**
"Your baby is doing well. I will visit you again on [Day 3, specific date]. On that day, I will check your baby's feeding, color, and cord care. If your baby develops fever, stops feeding, or has breathing problems before then, come to the health center immediately or call 108."

**At Visit 2:**
"Your baby is feeding better now. I will visit again on [Day 7, specific date]. Make sure the cord stays dry and clean. If you notice any fever, yellow color that gets worse, or if baby is not waking up to feed, tell me or come to the PHC."

**At Visit 3:**
"Your baby is growing and feeding is established now. I will visit next on [Day 14, specific date]. Your baby's weight is [weight and status — regaining, good, etc.]. Continue exclusive breastfeeding. Keep the cord area clean and exposed to air."

**At Visit 4:**
"Your baby is healthy and feeding well now. I will come for my last visit on [Day 28, specific date]. After that, you will take your baby to the ANM [health worker's name] at [location] on [date] for the vaccine. Remember exclusive breastfeeding until 6 months."

**At Visit 5:**
"Your baby's HBNC visits are now complete. Your next appointment is with the ANM at [location] on [date] for the 6-week vaccine and weight check. Continue exclusive breastfeeding and bring your baby to the PHC anytime if baby seems sick."

---

### **Danger Sign Followup Communication (Urgent Referral)**

**When referring to PHC same day:**
"Your baby has [specific finding - fever/infection/jaundice/feeding problem]. This needs to be checked by the doctor at the PHC. You should go today/this afternoon. Take your baby and come back to the health center [location]. Continue breastfeeding on the way and at the center. Tell the doctor about [relevant history]. After the doctor sees your baby, tell me what they said so I know how to help."

**When referring to PHC emergently (108):**
"Your baby has a serious problem and needs to go to the hospital right now. Call the ambulance number 108 or take your baby to [hospital name] immediately. Your baby needs help from the doctors there. You should go with your baby so you can breastfeed and comfort baby."

**When asking for a recheck visit sooner than standard:**
"Your baby has [mild concern - some jaundice/slight cord redness/feeding a bit slow]. This is not an emergency, but I need to check again in [2-3 days] to make sure it is improving. Come to my visit on [specific date and time]. Watch for these signs and tell me immediately if you see them: [list signs - fever, stops feeding, yellow color worse, etc.]. If baby gets worse before then, come to the PHC."

---

### **Weight-Related Followup Communication**

**If baby's weight is good:**
"Your baby's weight is good. Baby is regaining well from birth. Keep feeding baby 8-12 times every day with both breasts."

**If baby's weight is concerning:**
"Your baby's weight is lower than we want. This means baby may not be getting enough milk. Let me show you how to help baby feed better [demonstrate latch/position]. Feed baby more often — try 10-12 times every day. I will check again in [2-3 days] to see if the weight is improving."

**If baby is LBW:**
"Your baby is smaller than average, so we will check more carefully. I will visit on [Day 2 or 3] and then again on [Day 5]. This helps us make sure your baby is feeding well and growing. Come to every visit on time."

## Escalation Triggers: When to Change Scheduled Followup to Immediate Referral

Use these triggers to escalate a scheduled followup into an immediate (same-day or emergency) referral:

### **Escalation from "Monitor at Standard Schedule" to "Recheck in 2-3 Days"**

- **Mild concern becoming more obvious:** Slight redness now more red, mild jaundice becoming darker yellow
- **New danger sign appearing:** Baby was fine, now shows new concern (fever, lethargy, breathing change)
- **Mother reporting worsening:** "Baby seems worse" or "I'm very worried"
- **Multiple minor concerns together:** Baby has combination of minor issues (slight redness + poor feeding + mother stress)

**Action:** Do NOT wait for standard visit. Call mother and schedule recheck in 2-3 days.

---

### **Escalation from "Recheck in 2-3 Days" to "PHC Same Day"**

- **Sign not improving as expected:** Jaundice same or worse, redness spreading, feeding still inadequate at recheck
- **New danger sign appearing at recheck:** Baby was slightly yellow, now has fever
- **Vital sign abnormality:** Temperature elevated, breathing rate increased, lethargy worsening
- **Weight trajectory concerning:** Baby lost weight when should have been gaining, or weight loss accelerating

**Action:** Refer to PHC same day. Tell mother "Your baby needs the doctor today. Go to PHC now."

---

### **Escalation from Any Level to "108 Emergency Ambulance"**

- **Severe respiratory signs:** Severe indrawing, gasping, apnea, baby blue
- **Fever + lethargy:** Temperature ≥38.5°C and baby very sleepy/unresponsive
- **Shock signs:** Baby pale/mottled, weak pulse, very lethargic, cold extremities
- **Seizures or loss of consciousness:** Any seizure activity or baby unresponsive
- **Severe sepsis presentation:** Multiple signs together (fever + lethargy + fast breathing + poor feeding)
- **Severe cord infection:** Spreading cellulitis with fever and systemic signs
- **Severe jaundice:** Baby extremely yellow, lethargic, or with signs of bilirubin encephalopathy
- **Mother life-threatening emergency:** Severe bleeding, unconscious, thoughts of self-harm (in addition to continuing baby's care)

**Action:** Call 108 ambulance immediately. Do not waste time with PHC walk-in. Ensure mother accompanies baby.

---

### **Escalation Triggers Specific to Weight Issues**

- **Weight loss not stopping:** On Day 5-7, baby still losing weight (should have started regaining)
- **Weight loss >12%:** At any point, weight loss exceeding 12% from birth weight
- **No weight gain by Day 14:** Baby not approaching birth weight by Day 14 (concerning for inadequate feeding)
- **Failure to thrive trajectory:** Baby not gaining weight adequately at any followup visit

**Action:** Escalate to PHC for evaluation of feeding technique, milk supply, baby's ability to feed, or underlying condition.

---

### **Escalation Triggers Specific to Feeding Issues**

- **Complete feeding refusal:** Baby refusing all feeds, unable to latch, very weak suck
- **Feeding inadequacy with dehydration signs:** <6 wet diapers + poor skin turgor + lethargy
- **Mother unable to produce milk:** Breasts not filling, no let-down reflex by Day 5-7, and baby not gaining
- **Latch problems not improving with teaching:** After ASHA intervention, baby still cannot extract milk effectively

**Action:** Refer to PHC for lactation support, evaluation of mother/baby fit, or possible supplementation plan.

---

### **Escalation Triggers Specific to Cord Infection**

- **Redness spreading:** Cellulitis (redness extending >2 cm around cord site)
- **Discharge or odor appearing:** Any pus, foul smell, or oozing from cord
- **Fever appearing:** Temperature ≥38°C with cord signs
- **Systemic signs:** Fever + lethargy + poor feeding with cord signs

**Action:** 
- If spreading cellulitis + fever + systemic signs → 108 ambulance
- If spreading cellulitis or discharge with fever → PHC same day
- If spreading cellulitis but stable otherwise → PHC same day
- If mild redness worsening at recheck → escalate to PHC

---

### **Escalation Triggers Specific to Jaundice**

- **Worsening jaundice:** Jaundice deepening in color or spreading despite increased breastfeeding
- **Jaundice with lethargy:** Yellow color + baby sleepy/lethargic (risk of bilirubin encephalopathy)
- **Late-onset jaundice:** Jaundice appearing after Day 7-10 and worsening
- **Persistent jaundice:** Still very yellow on Day 14 without improvement

**Action:** 
- If jaundice + lethargy → PHC same day or 108 if baby appears seriously ill
- If worsening without lethargy → PHC same day
- If persistent but stable → PHC for assessment and likely phototherapy

## Summary: Decision Logic for Followup

**Every followup decision is based on this logic:**

1. **Assess baby's current status:** Vital signs, feeding, weight, appearance, danger signs
2. **Identify any danger signs:** Use the 6 core flags (breathing, jaundice, lethargy, cord, temperature, behavior)
3. **Determine severity:** Immediate emergency? Urgent same-day? Close monitoring? Standard? 
4. **Consider baby weight:** Is baby LBW or premature? Does this change the schedule?
5. **Determine next visit timing:** Based on severity + weight + visit number
6. **Communicate clearly:** Tell mother exactly when to come back and what signs mean she should come sooner
7. **Document:** Record the followup plan so it is clear to next visitor or PHC

**The golden rule:** When in doubt, schedule closer follow-up. It is better to see baby more often than miss a developing problem.
